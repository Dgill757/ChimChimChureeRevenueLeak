Built for This Industry

Built for Real-World
Chimney Operations

This is not a generic contractor calculator. The inputs and assumptions reflect how a CSIA-certified chimney and masonry company actually operates — not how a landscaper or plumber does.

Your work is highly specialized. Your operational pressure reflects that.

Running a chimney and masonry company means managing a technically demanding, seasonal, inspection-driven business where a $99 Level 1 call can uncover a $3,000 liner job — if you have the systems to follow it through.

The complexity stacks up fast: technician certifications, camera inspection reports, brick and mortar matching, gas line coordination, crown and chase cover assessment, prefab versus masonry decisions. Every stage of a chimney job involves handoffs, communication, and documentation that manual processes strain to keep up with.

The inputs in this calculator are calibrated to a chimney company running serious volume across Northern Virginia — not a one-truck handyman operation.

Common Chimney Services
and What They Typically Lead To

Small visits become large jobs — if the follow-up system is there to capture them.

Entry Service

Level 1 Visual Inspection — $99

A full visual walkthrough of everything accessible. Digital photos and inspection report included. Priced to get you in the door and start the diagnostic conversation.

Often surfaces: damper wear, cap or crown damage, mortar deterioration, or the need for a Level 2 camera inspection.
Diagnostic

Level 2 Video Inspection — $249–$319

Camera-based inspection that reveals what a visual pass cannot. Liner damage, cracks, blockages, hidden water intrusion paths — the problems that become expensive when missed.

Commonly leads to: liner installs ($1,800–$3,500), smoke chamber parging, or partial rebuild estimates.
Routine Maintenance

Fireplace Cleaning & Inspection — $229–$319

Seasonal service that keeps the customer relationship active year after year and surfaces repair needs before they become emergencies.

Frequently uncovers: chase cover deterioration, cap replacements, tuckpointing needs, damper issues.
High Ticket

Stainless Steel Liner Installation

One of the higher-ticket repair categories in your service mix. Almost always diagnosed through inspection first. Estimate-to-close requires a clean follow-up process to avoid losing jobs to competitors.

Market benchmark: $1,800–$3,500 depending on flue length and configuration.
